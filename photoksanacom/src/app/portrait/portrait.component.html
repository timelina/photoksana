Portrait

In a global sense, a portrait is a certain substance of a person’s life or of a certain period of it, reflecting thoughts, feelings, experiences, emotions, features of character, and even kind of activity. When I take portraits of people, my first priority is genuine interest, respect and love for the person portrayed: such an attitude is the foundation of mutual understanding between me and the model, allows me to tune in to work with a specific person, to feel his inner world, and then tell about it through the photo.

Working on portraits, I understand how important it is to be attentive to details, to notice and fix subtle features: the way you squint, frown, grin, raise an eyebrow, rub your palms in surprise. All this and much more, seen in time through the lens, will beautifully express, put the emotional background in the first place and make your photo “alive”.

Properly selected image will help to enhance the emotions of the photo as a whole and perfectly highlight your features. Remember that the main rule here is the following order: in the first place - you, in the second - what you are wearing. Clothing in any case should not over-pull the viewer's attention on itself, it is always an auxiliary element, only this way. The best option for almost any portrait is the basic clothing, consisting of things in which you are primarily comfortable.
